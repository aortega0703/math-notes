In a [[Field]] $F$, $a^{-1}$ is unique for all $a != 0  in F$.
##### Proof:
Let $a != 0 in F$ with multiplicative inverses $b$ and $c$, then
$$

	b &= 1b\
	&= (ca)b\
	&= c(ab)\
	&= c1\
	&= c

$$
Therefore $a^{-1}$ is unique
