In a [[Field]] $F$, $a^{-1}$ is unique for all $a\neq 0 \in F$.
##### Proof:
Let $a\neq 0\in F$ with multiplicative inverses $b$ and $c$, then
$$
\begin{align}
	b &= 1b\\
	&= (ca)b\\
	&= c(ab)\\
	&= c1\\
	&= c
\end{align}
$$
Therefore $a^{-1}$ is unique
