In a [[Group]] $G$, $a^{-1}$ is unique for all $a\in G$.
##### Proof:
Let $a\in G$ with inverses $b$ and $c$, then
$$
\begin{align}
	b &= eb\\
		&= (ca)b\\
		&= c(ab)\\
		&= ce\\
		&= c
\end{align}
$$
Therefore $a^{-1}$ is unique.
