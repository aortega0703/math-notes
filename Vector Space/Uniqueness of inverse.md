In a [[Vector Space]] $V$, $-v$ is unique for all $v\in V$.
##### Proof:
Let $v\in V$ with additive inverses $u$ and $w$, then
$$
\begin{align}
	u 
		&= u + \mathbf{0}\\
		&= u + (a + w)\\
		&= (u + a) + w\\
		&= 0 + w\\
		&= w
\end{align}
$$
Therefore $-v$ is unique.
