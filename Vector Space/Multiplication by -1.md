In a [[Vector Space]] $V$, $(-1) v = -v$ for all $v\in V$.
##### Proof:
Let $V$ a vector space and $v\in V$, then
$$
\begin{align}
	(-1)v + v 
		&= (-1)v + 1v\\
		&= (-1 + 1)v\\
		&= 0v\\
		&= \mathbf{0}\tag{1}\\
	(-1)v + v - v
		&= -v\\
	(-1)v 
		&= -v
\end{align}
$$
[^1]: uses [[Multiplication by 0 (scalar)]].
